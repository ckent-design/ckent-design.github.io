About Me

I am a student athlete from the United States, I am dedicated to developing my academic and athletic abilities. I currently attend The Browning School in New York City and I have a passion for athletics and psychology. I spend most of my time training as a goalkeeper who competes nationally and internationally. My goal for the future is to pursue collegiate soccer, play professional soccer, and become a sports psychologist. On the academic side I attend one of the top schools in New York City and am an Honor Roll student.

My experiences as a goal keeper has helped me understand the psychological demands of sports and has sparked my interest in the mental aspects of performance. As a social individual, I love building meaningful connections and meeting new people. In the future, I plan to use my athletic background and future studies in Sports Psychology to help guide athletes to improve and highlight the importance of the mental aspect in sports.
